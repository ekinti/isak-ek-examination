The Lighthouse is a 2019 film directed and produced by Robert Eggers, who co-wrote the screenplay with his brother, Max Eggers. The movie was released by the well renowned production company A24. It was an international co-production of the United States and Canada, with the film being shot in a very unconventional black-and-white color palette with a nearly square 1.19:1 aspect ratio. Willem Dafoe and Robert Pattinson star as two lighthouse keepers who begin to descend into madness when a storm strands them on the remote island where they are stationed. Director Eggers has stated that "I'm more about questions than answers in this movie.". For the most part, "The Lighthouse" takes place inside the mind of lighthouse keeper prodigy Ephraim Winslow, and through his weeks of agony on the lonesome volcanic rock the story becomes more and more hallucinatory. Even compared to other psychological thrillers this movie is avant garde, with scattered religious and ancient imagery all over the place.
